We know we can light a candle with a matchstick. But what if I say that we can re-ignite the extinguished candle from a distance. Curious right! In this demonstration, we've blown out a candle and reignited it by causing the flame to travel along a path of smoke. The method involved is not a mere magic trick but the scientific principle of thermodynamics.

Procedure of the experiment:-

  1. Light a candle. Have a second source of flame ready, such as another candle, a lighter, or a match.
  2. Blow out the candle and immediately place the lighted matchs into the smoke.
  3. The flame will travel down the smoke and relight your candle.

Tips for successful conduction of the experiment-

If you have trouble lighting the smoke, try moving your flame closer to the wick because that's where the concentration of vaporized wax is highest. Another tip is to make sure the air is still around the candle. Again, this is so you maximize the amount of wax vapor around the wick and have a clear smoke trail to follow.

How does the the travelling frame experiment work?

This experiment is based on how candles work. When you light a candle, the heat from the flame vaporizes the candle wax. When you blow the candle out, vaporized wax briefly remains in the air. If you apply a heat source quickly enough, you can ignite the wax and use that reaction to relight the wick of the candle. Although it looks like you're lighting the candle with smoke, it's really just the wax vapor that ignites. Soot and other debris from the flame aren't ignited.
